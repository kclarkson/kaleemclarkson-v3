What Kaleem Helps Your Audience Do

Strengthen social connection for remote and hybrid teams

Loneliness and disconnection are still the top struggles for remote employees, and most organizations have no real plan to fix it. In his talks Kaleem gives leaders practical social connection strategies that help people feel seen, supported, and part of a real team, wherever they work.

Design a remote employee experience that supports performance

Kaleem walks audiences through The Remote Employee Experience, a practical lifecycle framework that runs from attract and hire to offboard. Leaders leave with a clear map for how to improve onboarding, engagement, and development for distributed teams so performance and retention rise together.

Lead flexible work with confidence and clarity

Return to office debates can drain morale and trust. Kaleem helps executives reframe flexibility from a perk to a principle and gives them language, examples, and decision guides to align policies with culture, values, and business goals.

Social connection is the new office

For years, offices were treated as a shortcut for culture. Put everyone in the same building and relationships would take care of themselves. Remote work removed that shortcut and exposed a hard truth. Most organizations never had a real social connection strategy.

Kaleem Clarkson shows leaders how to design connection on purpose rather than rely on chance. Drawing on research about remote work struggles and loneliness, he explains why friendships, mentoring, and informal networks are now some of the most important drivers of performance and retention.

His keynotes blend stories from a decade of helping companies improve their remote employee experience with practical tools teams can start using the moment the event ends.

Signature Themes and Keynotes

The Remote Employee Experience (TREE)

How to design a people centered remote employee lifecycle that attracts, onboards, engages, and develops talent from anywhere. Audiences learn the TREE framework, identify gaps in their current experience, and leave with a short list of high impact changes they can make within ninety days.

Social Connection Strategy for Distributed Teams

Based on his talks and ongoing work with global clients, Kaleem shares a simple model for building colleague, mentor, and community connections in remote and hybrid environments. He shows how to move beyond virtual happy hours to build rituals, programs, and tools that actually deepen relationships and reduce isolation.

Leading Flexible Work Without Losing Your Culture

This session gives executives and people leaders a clear path through the noise around flexible work. Using real examples from clients and his own company, Kaleem explains how to align policies with values, design communication norms, and create a culture that supports both flexibility and accountability.

About Kaleem Clarkson

Kaleem Clarkson is a remote work advocate, people operations leader, and speaker who has spent more than a decade helping organizations design better remote employee experiences. He is the co founder and Chief Operating Officer of Blend Me, Inc, a consultancy that helps startups and small businesses become high functioning remote or hybrid workplaces.

Born in Bangor, Maine and based in Atlanta, Georgia, Kaleem is known for his practical, people centered approach to flexible work. He has been featured in Harvard Business Review, CNBC, and Fortune Magazine, named one of LinkedIn's Top Voices in Remote Work, and recognized as a top remote work advocate by major speaking bureaus.

He has spoken for audiences at SXSW, HP, Visa, Make A Wish Foundation, and many other organizations that care deeply about culture and connection. When he is not on stage, you can find him hosting conversations about remote work on his podcasts or advising leaders on how to make flexibility work in real life.
